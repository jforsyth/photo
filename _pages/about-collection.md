## About This Collection

This collection represents over a decade of passionate study and acquisition of ancient Greek and Roman coinage. The focus is on pieces that combine historical significance with artistic excellence and numismatic interest.

### Collection Philosophy

Rather than pursuing comprehensiveness, this collection emphasizes quality over quantity. Each piece is selected based on:

- **Historical Significance** - Coins that document important events, rulers, or transitions in ancient history
- **Artistic Merit** - Exceptional examples of die engraving and ancient artistic skill
- **Preservation** - Well-preserved specimens that showcase original design elements
- **Provenance** - Pieces with documented collecting history

### Areas of Focus

**Greek Coinage (5th-1st centuries BC)**
The collection includes representative examples of Classical and Hellenistic issues, with particular emphasis on Athenian tetradrachms and other city-state coinages.

**Roman Republic (3rd-1st centuries BC)**
Silver denarii documenting the political upheavals of the late Republic, featuring issues of famous generals and political figures.

**Roman Empire (27 BC - 235 AD)**
Gold and silver coinage from Augustus through the Severan dynasty, illustrating the evolution of imperial portraiture and propaganda.

### Research & Photography

All photographs on this site are original, captured using macro photography techniques to showcase the remarkable detail preserved in these ancient artifacts. Detailed descriptions draw on numismatic research and historical sources to provide context for each piece.
